**ODIDSI**

Odissi (Odia: ଓଡ଼ିଶୀ), also referred to as Orissi in older literature,
is a major ancient Indian classical dance that
originated in the Hindu temples of Odisha
– an eastern coastal state of India.[1][2][3]
Odissi, in its history, was performed predominantly by women,
[1][4]and expressed religious stories and spiritual ideas,
particularly of Vaishnavism. Odissi performances have also expressed ideas of other traditions
such as those related to Hindu Gods Shiva and Surya, as well as Hindu Goddesses (Shaktism).[5]
The theoretical foundations of Odissi trace to the ancient Sanskrit text Natya Shastra,
its existence in antiquity evidenced by the dance poses in the sculptures
of Odissi Hindu temples,[1][6] and archeological sites related to Hinduism (Sanatana Dharma),
Buddhism and Jainism.
